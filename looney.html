Looney Tunes

Written and Compiled by Matt Williams

1st Logo
(1930-1932)

Studio Logo: On a gray background, the words "WARNER BROS. PICTURES, INC." appeared, and below that "& THE VITAPHONE CORP." appears in a much smaller font, with the "VITAPHONE" using "electric" style letters. Below that is a very small WB shield which zooms up from a distance in the beginning (thus initiating a tradition), and in script, "Present". Behind it there is the drawing of a flag, "waving" so it looks like it is in 3 sections. On the first one, "WARNER BROS." Appears, followed by the electric-letter "VITAPHONE" logo and on section 3, "PICTURES". Below that is the copyright information.

Series Logo: A white sign in the middle has the words "LOONEY TUNES" and in black, "A HUGH HARMAN-RUDOLF ISING PRODUCTION" below that. Below the sign in small letters are the words "LEON SCHLESINGER, PRODUCER". Holding up the sign is Bosko, a Mickey Mouse-type character who was WB's current star at the time. Poking out from behind the sign and standing around the logo are stereotypical 30s cartoon animals.

End Title: Bosko stands in front of a sign saying "A LOONEY TUNE" and says "So Long, Folks!" Below it, in black, are the words "A HUGH HARMAN-RUDOLF ISING PRODUCTION", and "Licensed under BRAY-HURD patents".

SFX: No animation except for the small zooming shield.

Music: "A Hot Time In The Old Town Tonight" by Theodore Metz is the series theme.

Availability: Quite rare as Cartoon Network never reruns Bosko shorts due to their "ethnic offensiveness".

Scare Factor: None

2nd Logo
(1932-1933)

Studio Logo: Same as LT logo 1

Series Logo: Same as LT Logo 1, but this time the only animal is a bird, and helping Bosko hold up the sign is his girlfriend Honey.

End Title: Same as LT logo 1 except the lettering on the sign is in a different font and the "BRAY-HURD" text is in italics.

SFX: Not enough animation

Music: This time we have "Whistle And Blow Your Blues Away" by Carmen Lombardo & Joseph Young.

Availability: Again, very rare due to the reason listed above.

Scare Factor: None

3rd Logo
(1933-1935)

Studio Logo: Again, same as LT logos 1 and 2.

Series Logo: On a curtain backdrop to the right, WB's newest (and admittedly crappiest) cartoon star, Buddy, appears, holding his left hand up Vanna White-style. On the upper left side of the screen the words "LOONEY TUNES" appear and below Buddy and the curtain the words "PRODUCED BY LEON SCHLESINGER" appear.

End Title: Same as the Series Logo except Buddy is animated saying "So Long, Folks" and below the Leon Schlesinger credit the words "Distributed by WARNER BROS. PICTURES, INC."

SFX: As with LT Logos 1 and 2, there's not much animation. However, that was soon to change

Music: This time it's "Beauty And The Beast" by Bert Kalmar and Harry Ruby.

Availability: Rare again, but thankfully so as these cartoons are among the dullest in existence.

Scare Factor: None

4th Logo
(1935-1937)

Studio Logo: At the top of the screen, curved, the word "VITAPHONE" appears in the same electric letter font used previously, and on the very bottom is the word "Presents" in script, followed by the copyright info. The background is similar to a ship's porthole. And the WB Shield's most famous role is cemented-it zooms in from a long distance in the center of the screen to a huge size.

Variant: For the very first cartoons using the "porthole", a logo similar to LT logo 1 appears, but the "WARNER BROS. PICTURES" line is shortened to only "WARNER BROS." With "PRODUCTIONS CORPORATION" underneath it, then the "& The Vitaphone Corporation" line.

Series Logo: Using the same porthole background, in the center, "LOONEY TUNES" appears. The Beans Gang, WB's current stars, appear around it (When Porky became the LT star, this was altered accordingly to feature him). Below "LOONEY TUNES", "PRODUCED BY LEON SCHLESINGER" appears.

End Title: A black screen with "LOONEY TUNES" curved at the top-left with "PRODUCED BY LEON SCHLESINGER" on the bottom-right. "RELEASED BY WARNER BROS. PRODUCTIONS CORP." is at the very bottom, and at the center, the world-famous "That's All Folks!" logo appears.

Music: The music used for the previous logo was used at the beginning, but later on a "naval-like" flute theme is used.

SFX: The "writing on" of the "That's All Folks!", the infamous "zooming shield".

Availability: Can be seen on a few Beans' Gang LT shorts and the early Porky shorts.

Scare Factor: None really, the "zooming" shield didn't get scary until later on.

5th Logo
(1937-1943)

Studio Logo: Against a background of musical notes, the WB shield zooms in with "VITAPHONE" above and "PRESENTS" below. Later on, "VITAPHONE" is changed to "WARNER BROS." And "Presents" is changed to "Present".

Series Logo: "LOONEY TUNES" is curved near the top, with "PRODUCED BY LEON SCHESINGER" at the bottom. Porky Pig assumes a similar pose as Buddy did in Logo 3, and is on the left.

End Title: The same black screen as above until Bob Clampett's redesign of Porky Pig. Afterwards, his place in world history is assured as he bursts out of a drum saying his famous "Th-Th-Th-That's All Folks!" line. On the top of the drum is "LOONEY TUNES" and below it is "PRODUCED BY LEON SCHLESINGER". At the bottom is "RELEASED THRU WARNER BROS. PICTURES".

Music: Same as logo 4.

SFX: The "zooming shield".

Availability: Seen on many 30s and early 40s Porky Pig cartoons, though mostly colorized.

Scare Factor: None, again, the "zooming shield" didn't pose a problem until later.

6th Logo
(1943-1963)

Studio Logo: Similar to the previous logos, only now the famous "Circles/Bullseye" backdrop that has become a trademark of Warner Bros. Is in place. Below the "WARNER BROS.", "PICTURES INC." is added. And now the shield has its sound effect-the famous "twanging" sound created by Treg Brown.

Series Logo: Above the "bullseye" and on the same background, "LOONEY TUNES" appears in its now-distinctive font. Below it appears "PRODUCED BY LEON SCHLESINGER." In 1944, this was changed to "A WARNER BROS. CARTOON".

End Titles: Until 1945, the "Porky In A Drum" closing was used, however on some Bugs Bunny cartoons a variant was used where Bugs said "And That's The End!" while sitting in the open drum and munching on a carrot. Starting in 1944, the "LEON SCHLESINGER" text was changed to "A WARNER BROS. CARTOON". In 1945, a new closing was used. It started with the "That's All, Folks!" script being written out, and then "LOONEY TUNES" appearing at the top, curved as in the "black screen" logo. Near the bottom, either the Leon Schlesinger text/Released by WB combo was used (1943-44) or "A WARNER BROS. CARTOON" (1944-1963) was used. The background was the circles/bullseye used in the Studio Logo. The colors of the backdrop vary by year, but a list of the colors would be too long to put here. Instead, check out Dave Mackey's site here.

Variations: There were many variations to this logo, and here are some of them:

  • The most famous one of these, with Bugs Bunny relaxing on top of the shield as it zooms in. He chomps on his carrot for a few seconds, looks annoyed at the "camera", and then "pulls down" (like a curtain) the next logo, the Looney Tunes logo.
  • The shield fades into the character featured in the coming short. This was used mostly on Bugs Bunny cartoons, although other characters (Porky and Daffy, only Daffy, and only Porky) were used as well.
  • Some Looney Tunes were re-released as "Blue Ribbon" Merrie Melodies and lost their title cards. These re-releases kept the Looney Tunes music so it is painfully easy to spot former LTs that were reissued as Merrie Melodies.
  • Used on "Lumberjack Rabbit" only, the shield zooms in like normal, but overshoots its mark, crashing into the screen, before settling into the usual position. The reason for this was that the cartoon was originally produced in 3D, and this was Warner's most effective (and pretty much only, as the film made no good use of the 3D effect) use of the medium.

Music: The distinctive Looney Tunes theme, "The Merry-Go-Round Broke Down" is introduced. In the 1950s, this theme is shortened somewhat.

SFX: The "zooming shield".

Availability: Check Cartoon Network, this is on most of the Looney Tunes shown.

Scare Factor: High, mainly for the zooming shield and its VERY scary "TWANG!" sound effect.

7th Logo
(1963-1967)

Studio Logo: Completely different. Several series of lines come from the center of the screen, 3 purple, one yellow, with 2 of the purple ones diagonal, one of the purple ones vertical, and the yellow one horizontal. The yellow line moves up and down as the purple lines disappear and an abstract "WB", with the W made up of 2 triangles and the B made up of two semicircles, appears. The yellow line turns into the word "PRESENTS" over the abstract WB.

Series Logo: 2 lines from the center of the screen slide away to reveal a strange series logo. On the top is "LOONEY TUNES" in a weird font and on the bottom-right "A WARNER BROS. CARTOON" appears in a rectangle in that same font. Below the rectangle is the word "TECHNICOLOR". To the left of the rectangle is a bannerless WB shield. The lines then come back, slide back into each other, wiping away the text, and then "swirl" into the black background.

Early Variant: For the first cartoons with this logo, this text is on a white background with no WB shield. The sliding apart lines and the studio logo are still on a white background.

End Title: The abstract WB appears piece-by-piece, and "A WARNER BROS. CARTOON" is wiped onto the screen. When the wiping gets to the "OO" in "CARTOON", the Os turn yellow and "pop out" of the logo, then pop back into the logo, like two eyes doing a take. "N" is then wiped on and "A VITAPHONE RELEASE" and/or "A VITAGRAPH RELEASE" appear.

Music: A weird version of "The Merry-Go-Round Broke Down". The end titles originally used bells chiming when the WB appears, and then a honking sound for the OO animation.

Cheesy Factor: The "lines", really bad music and animation.

Availability: Seen on most mid-to-late 60s Looney Tunes on Cartoon Network.

Scare Factor: Medium, the music sucks and the animation is choppy. Also the abstract WB is creepy.

8th Logo
(1967-1969)

Studio Logo: The same as the previous logo, but the background is now blue and the 3 purple lines are now yellow and the yellow one is now pink. The 3 yellow lines now disappear at the same time, as the shield appears and the W7 logo "draws" itself in. The horizontal line animation is the same.

Series Logo: Again, same as last time, only the WB shield is dropped. The rectangle is now centered and reads "A WARNER BROS.-SEVEN ARTS CARTOON".

End Title: Same as last logo, although the "A WARNER BROS.- CARTOON" line is changed to add in the Seven Arts information and the abstract WB is replaced by the W7 logo.

Color: At least one or two Warner Bros.-Seven Arts cartoon bumpers appeared in more of a dark red color on occasion.

Music: Again, the same bad music used last time. But a few shorts may have the 43-63 theme played over, which does not fit in with the logo AT ALL.

Cheesy Factor: The "lines", again.

Availability: Quite rare; WB's cartoon output was coming to a stop by this time. But it is still saved on shorts whenever shown, but they are notoriously bad, so they aren't shown on the AOL networks often.

Scare Factor: A bit better, getting rid of the abstract WB and cleaning up the animation a bit.
