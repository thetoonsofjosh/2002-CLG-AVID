(DFS) Program Exchange

Written and Compiled by Jeffrey Gray

1st Logo
(1979-1987)

Nickname: "The Arrows"

Logo: On a black background, various glowing purple parallelograms zoom out from various parts of the screen. As more zoom out, we see that they are pieces of a 3-dimensional outline of the letters "dfs." As all the parallelograms zoom out to the top center of the screen, a 3-dimensional outline of a box zooms out, and takes its place in the top center of the screen, surrounding the "dfs." Once everything is in place, the black background dissolves to a blue-to-white gradient background with a pattern of black dots in it. The "dfs" and box are white, and the area inside the box, surrounding the letters, is blue. A left-pointing arrow with a large triangular section, the rectangular section near the top of the triangular section, and the word "PROGRAM" inside the rectangular section, comes in from the right side of the screen under the logo. At the same time, a right-pointing arrow, exactly like the left-pointing arrow, except with the rectangular section near the bottom of the triangular section, and "EXCHANGE" in the rectangular section, comes in from the right side of the screen under the logo. They cross each other, then stop in a position where the two rectangular sections are stacked right on top of each other, the logo is stacked right on top of the rectangular sections, and the triangular sections of the arrows jutting out of the rectangle which the logo and the rectangular sections of the arrows have formed.

SFX: The parallelograms zooming out, the arrows moving across the screen.

Cheesy Factor: The effect of the parallelograms zooming out isn't all that cheesy, but the effect of the arrows moving across the screen is incredibly cheesy.

Music: A bizarre synth tune comprised of two separate, continuously rising and falling synth tunes.

Availability: Nearly Extinct, can sometimes be seen on "The Abbott and Costello Show" on TV Land.

Scare Factor: Median, mainly due to the music

2nd Logo
(1987-)

Nickname: "The Crossing Arrows/Lines"

Logo: On a blue background with black lines running through it, we zoom out on two sets of blue words with silver lines running through them, "PROGRAM" and "EXCHANGE," and two stylized silver arrows, which come from different corners of the screen and all cross paths in an "X" formation before reaching their final areas of the screen and stopping. We see a small "THE" above the logo and "A Division of Saatchi and Saatchi-DFS, Inc." below it. The logo "shines."

Byline: In later years, the byline appears as "A Division of Saatchi and Saatchi North America"

SFX/Cheesy Factor: The entire formation of the logo, the "shine."

Music: First used a remix of the DFS tune, but would later switch to an unrelated, lilting dreamy tune.

Availability: The first variation can be seen on Rocky and Bullwinkle on Cartoon Network and Amen on BET, and the second variation can be seen on Sailor Moon on Cartoon Network and Garfield and Friends on Nickelodeon.

Scare Factor: Median, the animation will only annoy people, but the original music has been referred to as "eerie," and has even frightened some.
