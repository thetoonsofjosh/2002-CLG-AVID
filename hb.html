Hanna-Barbera Productions

Compiled by Eric Hartman, Jason Jones, James Fabiano and Matt Williams

(Note: This page will focus on the actual, ending logos of Hanna-Barbera, not the one used on cartoon shorts. Those will be described at a later time.)

1st Logo
(1966-1974)

Nickname: "The Box"

There were 3 versions of this logo, and they will be described here.

Logo 1 (early prototype, 1966): Over a black background, three orange lines trail (the outer 2 going down, the one in the center going up), then they break into small rectangles forming a box containing a stylized HB. The box then becomes an orange background, as the HB zooms out to the near center. The HB turns into a black box, and the phrase "a hanna-barbera production" pops in afterward. (The "a" appears inside the black box) Finally, the stylized HB fades in underlapping the company text.

Logo 2 (standard version): Over a black background with a quick glide up the musical scale, a yellow stylized HB zooms in to fill the screen. The HB stops and turns orange over a yellow background under the phrase "a hanna-barbera production, a division of taft broadcasting company". A small orange box appears behind the "a" in the company text. The Taft company logo, with the words Progress-Public-Trust appears next to the Taft byline, which was seen between 1968 and 1971.

Logo 3 (different variation, 1969-1971): Over a black background, three red mini rectangles trail (the outer 2 going down, the one in the center going up), then they multiply into more rectangles forming a box containing a stylized HB. The box then becomes a red background, as the HB fades to light blue. The phrase "a hanna-barbera production, A Taft BROADCASTING COMPANY" fades in while the HB changes its color. A small pink-outlined box with purple inside appears behind the "a" in the company text. The Taft company logo appears within the Taft byline this time.

(Note: The bylineless variation is the most common, and can be seen currently on Cartoon Network/Boomerang shows with this logo)

SFX/Cheesy Factor: The "box covering screen" effect.

Music: The early rendition of the logo features a fast-paced sting of a chime-driven fanfare. The regular logo has a musical scale glide with a brass band and then lower notes are played. The latter sounder was dropped in 1971 and the logo was eventually played under the music outro of a series' end titles.

(Note: "The New Scooby Movies" and "SuperFriends" were among those that played their theme music over the logo.)

Availability: Can still be seen on many H-B cartoons on Cartoon Network and Boomerang like "Scooby-Doo," "Super Friends" and "Wacky Races". The early prototype appeared at the end of the 1966 NBC special "Jack and the Beanstalk" starring Gene Kelly.

Scare Factor: High, it has caused nightmares with the "slide" music and terrible logo design with abruptness.

2nd Logo
(1974-1979)

Nickname: "Rainbow H-B"

Logo: Five columns of rainbow-colored "Hanna-Barberas" can be seen, then they disappear two at a time; first columns 1 (which is blacked out downward) and 5 (which is blacked out upward), and then 2 (upward) and 4 (downward). The middle column is left against a black background, and it shrinks away into a single "Hanna-Barbera," which "blossoms" into into a stylized "H-B" with a scrolling rainbow pattern filled with "Hanna-Barberas." Below the logo are the words:

HANNA-BARBERA PRODUCTIONS INC.
A TAFT BROADCASTING COMPANY

(Note: The Taft byline was added in 1978)

(Note: There was another variation of this logo, which had rainbow segmented lines running horizontally inside the static HB design. This appeared on a few late '70s TV movies from the company.)

(Note: The normal logo came in two sizes: A larger one with no byline was used from 1974-1978, after which a smaller one with the Taft byline came into use. As it did with other logos, Great American had the byline of the latter blacked out. )

SFX: Columns disappearing, the logo "blossoming."

Cheesy Factor: The disappearing was done by a blacking out effect.

Music: Usually had the ending theme of whatever cartoon played over the logo. Never had its own music.

Availability: Uncommon, can still be found on many 70s H-B shows played on Boomerang like Jabberjaw and Hong Kong Phooey.

Scare Factor: Minimal, it mainly depends on the ending theme of the cartoon shown

3rd Logo
(1979-1992, 1998- )

Nicknames: "Shining Star", "Twisting Star", "Swirling Star", "CGI Swirling Star"

Logo: Starts out with a white star moving in a circular path, leaving a residue trail of many colors. Then, the star is in the middle of the trail, stops, and the Hanna-Barbera corporate name appears. A computer generated rendition of the logo was introduced on network TV in 1986.

(Note: The logo used to have the words "A Taft Broadcasting Company" [1979-1981], and "A Division of The Taft Entertainment Company" [1981-1988] below Hanna-Barbera, but has been blacked out for the most part starting in 1988.)

(Note: There are 2 variations of the logo, brighter colors where used in 1986-1992 compared to the duller, faded out colors from 1979-1986.)

(Note: In 1988, a superimposed logo with "Hanna-Barbera" in a script typeface with the star logo was first shown on episodes of "The Smurfs")

(Note: In 1990 to celebrate the 30th anniversary of "The Flintstones", Fred Flintstone "danced" around the logo in the 3rd note.)

(Note: A version was used to open movies and TV shows that had "Hanna-Barbera" over the trail and Presents below)

(Note: On some current shows, such as The Powerpuff Girls, the text "A Time Warner Company" is chyroned on)

SFX: The animated star and residue trail, the music.

Music: Said to be described as "futuristic synthesized music".

(Note: The music used for the 68-74 ID was heard over this logo on Scooby Doo reruns on TBS and Boomerang, and on Josie and The Pussycats on Cartoon Network's Boomerang)

(Note: The closing credits of "Jetsons: The Movie" has a variation of the first Swirling Star logo design with the cursive "Hanna-Barbera" from note 3 and a byline for their then-owner "A Great American Broadcasting Company". Music for this variation was a majestic instrumental rendition of the "Meet George Jetson" part of the Jetsons theme.)

Availability: Seen on lots of H-B cartoons on Cartoon Network that originally aired in the 80s.

Scare Factor: Minimal, the music might scare some, but the logo has a large following.

H-B Production Co./Turner
(1992-1993?)

Logo: In this still logo, we see a strange shaded background that consists of 3 circles (kinda like the Looney Tunes ones, but less obvious) of (outer to inner) blue, light green, and solid green. On this background is the following text:

A
H-B Production Co.
and
Turner Entertainment Co.
Cartoon

The text is all in white and centered. There is some spacing between lines as well. The H-B text is in a cursive/paintbrush type font, while the Turner text is in a plain, blocky font, similar to end credits used on some Marvel shows of the 80s. To the left of the Turner line is an abstract star logo, made by a border of parallelograms, and almost resembling the common "recycling" logo seen in the US.

(Note: An earlier version exists. It's almost the same, only it has a still, colorless version of the swirling star and "Hanna-Barbera" above the Turner logo.)

SFX: None, it's a still logo

Cheesy Factor: Just a gripe, but why would Hanna-Barbera submit to having their company name initialized?

Music: None, just the end theme to "Tom & Jerry Kids"

Availability: As far as I know, was only shown on the "Tom & Jerry Kids" series. Up until a few years ago, it was seen on Cartoon Network and a few local stations. So whenever someone wants to run the show, this logo should remain intact.

Scare Factor: The bouncy theme makes it low, but if this logo was ever used w/o music, it might rise a bit due to the strange font and text considerations.

4th Logo
(1993-1997)

Nickname: "All-Stars"

Logo: Has a character from the cartoon shown in an oval or rectangle under the Hanna-Barbera script typeface logo. There was no animation during the first year. One variation could be seen on "The Addams Family" where Uncle Fester is in the rectangle. Quick-cuts of cartoon characters from past and present were introduced in 1994. There are two variations, one for comedy (i.e. "2 Stupid Dogs"), the other for action (i.e. "Swat Kats", "Real Adventures of Johnny Quest"). The quick-cuts end on a "boing". The comedy one ends on Fred Flintstone and the action one ends on Johnny Quest. "A Turner Company" is shown on the bottom.

SFX: The many appearances of various cartoon characters, leading up the last.

Music: Just full of standard sound effects and a "boing" at the end. The un-animated ID occasionally has the ending theme of whatever cartoon playing over it.

Availability: Common, can be seen on all mid 90s H-B shows and many from the 60s on Cartoon Network.

Scare Factor: None, nothing is creepy and it's neat to see all the characters.

5th Logo
(1997-2001)

Logo: Like the 1993 logos, this one has a character from the cartoon shown (i.e. Dexter in Dexter's Laboratory, I.R Baboon in I.M Weasel) in an oval or rectangle that says Hanna-Barbera in the same script typeface as logo 9. There is no animation. Says "A Time Warner Company" on the bottom.

SFX: None, it's a still logo

Music: Basically a bunch of standard "boing" sound effects.

Availability: Common, seen on most of the "Cartoon Cartoons" on Cartoon Network.

Scare Factor: None

Cartoon Network Studios
(2001-)

Generic Logo: On a white background, we see a very "grungy" variation on the "Cartoon Network" logo constantly flashing on the screen. A vertical chartreuse light cursor moves from left to right "printing" the CN logo in its place. The cursor moves from right to left again, the CN logo flashes some more, then the cursor moves once more to the right printing the checkered text "STUDIOS" below the logo.

Logo Variations: Like the MTM logo, the basic logo described above is often customized for the show it appears on. Basically, the Cartoon Network Studios logo described above appears on a background, the "CARTOON NETWORK" above and "STUDIOS" below opens away from each other vertically like a mouth, the characters do their act, then the logo closes again. However, the characters in the logo vary depending on the show. Here are the variations:

  • Time Squad: From left to right stand Tudrussell, Otto, and Larry. Tudrussell waves his finger to Larry as a signal. Larry looks to the button on his right and pushes it. The characters disappear in a zap of light.
  • Grim and Evil: From left to right stands Mandy, Billy, and Grim. Grim is holding a scythe. With a wicked grin on his face, he swings his scythe in an effort to "behead" Billy and Mandy, but the kids hide under their shirts and he misses.
  • Samurai Jack: Jack holds his sword up in front of his face, leans his body to the left posing the sword like a baseball bat, and quickly swings the sword right and left breaking the lip of the Cartoon Network Studios logo, which promptly closes.
  • Dexter’s Laboratory: Dee Dee stands on the left with her finger pointed at a button, and Dexter on the right gets scared as Dee Dee pushes the button. An exploding flash of light is seen and Dexter emerges from it scorched all over.

SFX: Depends on the variation

Music: Sound effects that vary depending on the logo variation.

Availability: Brand new. Variations currently seen on the "Cartoon Cartoons" listed above; the generic logo is seen on "The Cartoon Cartoon Show."

Scare Factor: None

Southern Star/Hanna-Barbera Australia
(Used throughout the 80s primarily)

Nickname: "Cheesy Lightning"

Logo: On a blue space background, a spinning blue star twirls out. It plasters itself firmly at the center of the screen, while a gray lightning bolt slides in from the top-left behind the star, landing so that the star is in the middle of the lightning bolt. The screen flashes a few times and two rectangles slide in from the top and bottom of the screen, behind the star and lightning bolt, the top one yellow, the bottom one purple. When they connect with each other, the colors suddenly swap so that the top one is purple and the bottom one is yellow. In the purple rectangle, to the right of the star/bolt contraption, is "A SOUTHERN STAR" in a yellow-colored thin italic font, and in the yellow bottom rectangle is "Hanna Barbera Australia Production" in purple and the font normally used for "Hanna-Barbera Productions" on the swirl logo. "©<YEAR> TAFT-HARDIE GROUP PTY LIMITED" appears below the logo.

SFX: The star zooming out, the bolt, the rectangles

Cheesy Factor: Dear lord, this is off the charts. The star zooming out is cheesy, the animation for the bolt is almost TOO simple, the flashing is just plain awful, and need I mention the suddenly swapping colors for the rectangle? Ugh...

Music: The ending of the show's theme, but with a very loud thunder sound effect edited in with the flash.

Availability: This will probably never be seen again. It was only on "Teen Wolf" and "Berenstain Bears", and to my knowledge, this logo wasn't on recent reruns of the former show. The latter show will probably never be shown again on TV.

Scare Factor: The "thunder" effect will scare more than a few.
